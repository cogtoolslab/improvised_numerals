Tagging Symbols

Welcome! In this experiment, you will play a game where you view a series of 70 drawings and tell us what features they have. Each drawing was made by someone who was playing a Pictionary-style game, where their goal was to produce a drawing to convey how many objects there were in an image, without using standard numerals. The drawings will be shown at the top of the screen, along with the sets of animals they are describing. We need your help identifying what strategies these people used when producing their drawings. There are five main strategies: matching, grouping, numbers, clipping, and unique.

You might be interested to learn that these are real strategies that people in different cultures have used throughout history to communicate about number! Let’s walk through what each of these strategies means.

Types of Numerals

1: Matching. A drawing is using a matching strategy whenever one mark in the drawing represents one object, as seen in the example below. Hover your mouse over the image to count the marks and bears!

2: Grouping. A drawing is using a grouping strategy whenever one mark in the drawing represents multiple objects, as seen in the example below. Hover over the image to see grouping in red, and matching in orange.

3: Clipping. A drawing is using a clipping strategy if there are some objects in the target image that are “missing” in the drawing. In other words, the marks in the drawing represent some, but not all, of the objects in the target image. 5: Unique. A drawing is using a unique strategy if it is not clear to you how the marks relate to the objects in the target image, perhaps because whoever produced the drawing had invented some different way of representing number information. Drawings that cannot be described by the other features should be marked as unique.



Important: Any individual drawing may use more than one of these five strategies. Your job is to indicate any and all of the strategies you believe were used. If you are not sure, please provide your best guess! There will be six practice trials to get the hang of it before starting for real.
